A* is the most popular choice for pathfinding, because it’s fairly flexible and can be used in a wide range of contexts. In this project I used it to find a shortest path.

One important aspect of A* is f = g + h.


The f, g, and h variables are in our Node class and get calculated every time we create a new node. Quickly I’ll go over what these variables mean.
F is the total cost of the node. G is the distance between the current node and the start node. H is the heuristic — estimated distance from the current node to the end node.
